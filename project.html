Actual tar sand is used to print various images. The texture hereof stands as a tangible memento for a landscape lost by destruction. Some of these prints have the sand scraped off, leaving only a ghostly trace of a landscape.

Other prints have used the dull black carbon of petro coke (a heavy residue from the oil refining process), depicting the rational industry at work. The installation is tied together by accompanying texts from Man and Nature by George P. Marsh.

He was the first to write about man-made climate change, and the discomfort that comes with irreversible consequences, but also voicing the excitement of technological development and the resilience of nature.
